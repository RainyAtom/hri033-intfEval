Interface Evaluation Task Instructions

In this study, you will help us evaluate two interfaces intended for tracking the components of multiple robots. Below are gifs of what these interfaces look like.
Each gif demonstrates multiple robots (Robot A, B, C, and D) connecting to an interface. Individual robots are composed of multiple disparate components. These components are represented either by a label like “CameraComponent1” (as shown in interface 1) or by icons like a camera silhouette (as shown in interface 2). Either connecting lines (interface 1) or visual grouping of components within a rectangular space (interface 2) are used to indicate which components belong to which robot.

To evaluate these interfaces, you will take part in 3 rounds of tasks per interface (6 rounds total, two of which are practice rounds). During each round, you will have two panels to provide attention to.
These panels will be placed side-by-side within the same window as shown in the image below. On the left-side is PanelA which will display a video of one of the interfaces shown above. On the right-side is PanelB which will contain a letter counting task.



For each round you will need to do the following:
  1. Before beginning a round/video, familiarize yourself with the initial configuration of the interface shown in PanelA.
  2. To begin a round, press "Play Video" in PanelA. The video demonstrates one of the interfaces shown above. You’ll need to passively pay attention to the video shown and any changes that may occur.
  3. While the video plays, you’ll need to work on a task in PanelB. Your task is to look through a sample of text and count the number of times the letter ‘a’ appears within the text.
  4. Once the video finishes, you’ll be asked to answer questions about the video shown in PanelA. Note: The video cannot be replayed and is about 1 minute in length.
  5. You will also then have the chance to provide a number response for the task in PanelB.
Before moving onto the next round, all questions must be answered.
Once all rounds are complete, you will be asked a final open question about the interfaces demonstrated. Make sure to click through to the end of the study to receive your Prolific Completion Code.
